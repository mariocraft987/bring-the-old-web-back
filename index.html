Story of the old net

In 1989, a british scientist named Tim Berners Lee created the World Wide Web, WWW for short.
A few years later, there were a variety of web browsers, netscape being one of them. In 1994,
Beverly Hills Internet was made, it was a web hosting site, it was then renamed to GeoCities. Due
to the '80s through '90s being a colorful time, sites were very asthetic and personalized.
GeoCities was then sold to Yahoo! and shut down, freezing all of the "Under Construction" sites.
